Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers try to steal sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or personal data by pretending to be a trusted source. This usually happens through emails, messages, or fake websites.

In a typical phishing attack, the victim receives a message that looks like it is from a bank or a well-known company. The message contains a link and asks the user to log in or verify their account. When the user clicks the link, they are taken to a fake website that looks real. Once they enter their details, the attacker gains access to their information.

Students and working professionals are common targets because they frequently use online services. The consequences can be serious, including financial loss, identity theft, and unauthorized access to accounts.

For example, a student may receive a message claiming to be from a payment app asking to verify their account. If they enter their details, their bank account may be compromised.

This shows how important it is to be cautious while interacting online and to always verify the authenticity of messages before taking any action.
